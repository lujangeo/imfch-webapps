Who we are

A school where CHamoru is the language of learning.

Faniyåkan Sinipok CHamoru Immersion School provides full CHamoru medium/immersion education for Pre-K through 6th grade. All subjects are taught through CHamoru — building fluency naturally while meeting GDOE academic standards. Students also have access to Library, Counseling, GATE, After School, and Summer School programs.

Inetnon Manaina Faniyåkan Sinipok is our parent–teacher nonprofit — the community backbone supporting advocacy, programming, and family co-learning. The CHamoru Studies Division and Faniyåkan CHamoru Immersion Program are ADA Compliant.

An independent CHamoru immersion school on the P.C. Lujan Elementary campus in Radio Barrigada — with our own identity, enrollment, and community.

Frequently asked questions

What parents ask most

Most common concern

Research consistently shows that immersion students perform at or above grade level in English — often outperforming peers in reading and literacy. Building strong foundations in CHamoru actively supports English language development. Faniyåkan students meet all GDOE academic standards.

Enrollment requirement

Yes — this is exactly what our program is built for. Faniyåkan offers parent classes designed for non-speakers to learn alongside their children. Our upcoming Parents Portal will include co-learning tools for home use. You don't need to speak CHamoru to enroll — you need to be willing to learn.
All subjects — math, science, reading, social studies — are taught through CHamoru from day one. Students absorb the language naturally. Classes are smaller, the community is close-knit, and CHamoru culture is woven into everything we do, not treated as a separate subject.

Participation requirement

In-kind service includes volunteering at events, helping in the classroom, supporting administrative tasks, participating in cultural activities, and contributing to community programs. The school works with families to find service that fits their schedule and skills. Contact Tan Jocelyn Santos for a full list of qualifying activities.
Weekly family classes are structured CHamoru language sessions for parents and guardians — designed for beginners, no prior knowledge required. Family engagement expectations grow as students advance through grade levels, so starting in Pre-K or Kinder gives your family the best foundation. Contact the school for current session times.
Transportation details depend on your village and GDOE busing routes. Contact Tan Jocelyn Santos at jtsantos@gdoe.net to discuss options for your family. School hours are 8:30 a.m. – 2:43 p.m.

ADA compliant

Faniyåkan Sinipok is an ADA Compliant program under the CHamoru Studies Division of GDOE. Families with children who have IEPs or special education needs are encouraged to contact us directly so we can discuss how services can be coordinated. Every child's situation is considered individually.
Faniyåkan Sinipok accepts enrollment from Pre-K through 6th grade. Our marketing focus is on Pre-K and Kindergarten because early childhood immersion delivers the greatest language development benefit — and because enrollment for younger grades requires less prior family engagement to qualify. Families interested in grades 1–6 are welcome to contact us directly.

Limited seats

Enrollment is limited to keep class sizes small and ensure personalized attention. Spots are filled on a first-come, first-qualified basis — early applications are strongly encouraged. Contact Tan Jocelyn Santos for current seat availability and selection criteria.
